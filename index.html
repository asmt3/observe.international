Observe is a group of lawyers and legal specialists dedicated to supporting and promoting rule of law and human rights projects worldwide.

WHAT IS THE RULE OF LAW?

The rule of law, in its most basic form, is the principle that no one is above the law.

It is the principle that everyone, from the individual to the state, is accountable to the law, and that there is a clear set of laws that are publicly stated and equally enforced. Critical to the protection of the rule of law is an independent judiciary that properly safeguards individuals from the arbitrary use of power by the state, organisations or other individuals.

Establishing respect for the rule of law is fundamental to the foundation of peaceful communities, the effective protection of human rights and to sustained economic development and progress.

A lack of the rule of law can be found in both democracies and dictatorships.

OBSERVE'S AIMS

  • To support and promote a wider application of the rule of law and human rights across the globe.
  • To review and assess whether rule of law and human rights principles are being respected
  • To provide training on all rule of law and human rights issues
  • To produce high quality studies on rule of law and human rights issues
  • To highlight threats to the rule of law and human rights

Rosa Curling

Rosa Curling is a UK and international human rights lawyer. She is at the forefront of claimant litigation, and has represented clients before the High Court, Court of Appeal, Supreme Court, European Court of Justice, European Court of Human Rights, International Criminal Court, as well as many domestic and international tribunals. Read more...

She has acted for clients in challenges to numerous UK national bodies, on a variety of different issues including public international law, international criminal law, health, education, corporate social responsibility, the environment, unlawful detention, freedom of information, the right to privacy, right to life, and the right to die. Some of her recent cases and press reports are listed below.

She is currently a trustee of Platform, a co-founder of Observe and a member of European Mediterranean Human Rights Network's Working Group on Freedom of Association and Assembly.

She has written and provided comment on a wide range of human rights issues in the national and international press as well as numerous conferences, seminars and other events. She recently wrote a chapter on the freedom of assembly and association in EMHRN's Regional Study on the freedom in the Euro-Mediterranean region.

She regularly provides training on human rights and public law issues both in the UK and elsewhere and currently provides five training sessions a year, in association with the Consultation Institute, on the law of consultation. She has undertaken both trial and demonstration monitoring missions in the UK and elsewhere.

She was previously been named the Times Lawyer of the Week and was shortlisted for Liberty Human Rights Lawyer of the Year 2014-2015.

Catherine Fischl

Catherine Fischl is a UK based criminal lawyer working on a wide variety of criminal cases, the majority of which have an international element. Read more...

She acts for clients under investigation by the police and other UK prosecutors, and has represented clients being prosecuted in the UK Crown and Magistrates’ Courts and in cases litigated before the Court of Appeal, the High Court, the European General Court and the European Court of Justice. She regularly makes representations on behalf of her clients to a wide range of national and international government agencies including INTERPOL and the European Council.

As an extradition lawyer, Cath represents clients who are the subject of often politically motivated extradition requests made to the UK by foreign governments. Such cases involve careful review of the human rights position in the requesting country in relation to the right to life, the right to a fair trial and the right to liberty and security of the person.

Cath writes on criminal law related issues for various publications. She has acted as a trial monitor, has designed and presented training on the right to a fair trial and has experience of conducting workshops in relation to bribery and corruption issues in Europe, Russia and South-East Asia.

She is a co-founder of Observe and a member of the Association of Commonwealth Criminal Lawyers and the Young Fraud Lawyers Association.

Zoe Norden

Zoe Norden is a media law specialist currently working in-house for a global media organisation. Read more...

She has experience advising on a range of content-related matters including defamation, privacy, contempt of court, the official secrets act, PACE applications and reporting restrictions imposed in this jurisdiction and elsewhere. She also provides pre-publication advice and training to journalists carrying out multi-jurisdictional investigations. For example she has been involved in advising on matters arising out of Wikileaks coverage, the Leveson Inquiry, phone hacking reporting and on journalist investigations into undercover police officers.

She has represented clients before the High Court, Commercial Courts, Court of Appeal, Supreme Court and ECHR. Zoe also engages in challenging reporting restrictions imposed in other areas, such as Family and Coroners Courts. She was instrumental in the challenge by UK media organisations in what were described as unprecedented reporting restrictions imposed on journalists during a criminal trial relating to alleged terrorism related offences.

She also co-ordinates and oversees media challenges on open justice principles on behalf of the Media Lawyers Association and engages in government consultations in areas which impact the media. This includes playing an active role in challenging proposed changes to the law in particular in the areas of contempt and archives.

Zoe was a member of the Working Group formed by the Civil Justice to look at the issue of costs in defamation proceedings. She is a committee member of the Media Lawyers Association and member Media Law Resource Centre.

Jan Clements

Jan Clements is a media law specialist and the current Senior Legal Adviser for Guardian News & Media Limited. She advises GNM on issues such as defamation, privacy, contempt of court, the official secrets act and reporting restrictions. She has conduct of GNMS’s post publication litigation. Read more...

Jan is an expert in public, human rights, freedom of information and data protection law, has led media challenges to secret trials and has lobbied government on the acquisition of communications data, police powers, access to courts and open justice.

She is a member of the Media Lawyers Association, Media Law Resource Centre and co-author of "Regulating journalism and newsgathering" in "Media Law and Practice" OUP. She has trained journalists, and has spoken at national and international conferences on all of these issues.

Jan is also actively engaged in human rights issues in southern Africa. She is currently on the National Executive Committee of Action for Southern Africa. She was a founding member of the Anti-Apartheid Women’s Committee and Secretary of the London Anti-Apartheid Committee that coordinated the activities of local London Anti-Apartheid groups.

In 1984 she organised, with Archbishop Trevor Huddleston, an interfaith colloquium on apartheid and later joined the staff of the International Defence and Aid Fund to support the families of political prisoners in South Africa, visiting Robben Island in the early 1990s to assess the needs of prisoners on their release.
